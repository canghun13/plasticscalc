Reference density: verify the exact grade

Density is suitable for converting a finished part’s measured or CAD volume to mass. The figures below are manufacturer-published solid-density references, not melt-density values for barrel/shot-volume calculations.

Material gradeDensityBasis
Ultramid B3S Q661 (PA6)1.13 g/cm³ISO 1183; BASF product data
Ultradur B 4300 G6 HR (PBT-GF30)1.51 g/cm³ISO 1183; BASF product data, typical uncoloured value at 23°C

Fillers, moisture, colour, temperature, and grade can change density. Enter the value from the exact material data sheet or a validated part measurement. Do not copy these solid-density figures into the Shot Volume Calculator, which asks for melt density.

Data selection workflow

Match the density state to the decision.

Do not move a density value from a data sheet into a different physical-state calculation without recording its grade, test basis, and temperature context.

Finished part mass

Use CAD or measured volume with an exact-grade solid density. Validate against a repeatable weighed molded part when one is available.

Injection-unit volume

Use complete shot mass with a documented melt-density basis. The solid-density references above are not a barrel-capacity input.

Filled and conditioned grades

Do not substitute an unfilled family value for a filled grade. Record material condition and use the specific supplier data sheet or a validated part measurement.

Material cost

Use weighed part and runner mass, purchase price, cavity allocation, and an explicit recovery/scrap rule. Density is a cross-check, not a price input by itself.

The density rows above are deliberately limited to cited grade-specific examples. For a new grade, retrieve the supplier data sheet first; do not infer a value from a polymer-family average.
